Why every wedding videographer needs a client questionnaire

Weddings are high-stakes, one-shot events. You cannot ask the bride on the day of the ceremony whether she wants audio from the vows or who her videographer contact is.

A wedding videographer client questionnaire fixes three expensive problems:

  • **Scope creep**. When the client asks for an extra shooter or same-day edit later, you point to what was agreed.
  • **Missed shots**. You know the surprise first look, the memorial tribute, and the exact grand-exit plan before the day starts.
  • **Delivery disputes**. You confirm format, length, music rules, and turnaround time in advance.

Most wedding filmmakers lose clients not because of bad footage, but because of a missed expectation. The questionnaire prevents that.

When to send the wedding video questionnaire

Send the first questionnaire four to six weeks before the wedding, when the couple still has bandwidth to think through details.

If it is a destination wedding or a large multi-day event, send it eight weeks out.

Send a shorter confirmation form one week before the wedding to catch last-minute changes: vendor contacts, timeline shifts, and weather backup plans.

How to use this wedding videography questionnaire

This list is intentionally thorough. Most couples will not answer every question. That is fine.

The goal is to make them think through details they would otherwise forget.

Best practice:

1. Copy the questions into your CRM, Google Form, or HoneyBook questionnaire.

2. Remove sections that do not fit your package.

3. Add your own branding and delivery notes.

4. Send it with a deadline.

5. Follow up once if you do not hear back within one week.

Section 1: Wedding day logistics

These questions remove the basic confusion that derails wedding films.

1. What is the full date and day of the week of your wedding?

2. What are the ceremony start time and end time?

3. What is the full address of the ceremony venue?

4. What is the full address of the reception venue?

5. Are the ceremony and reception at the same location?

6. What time will you be getting ready?

7. Where will you be getting ready? Full address if different from the venue.

8. Will the groom or second partner be getting ready at a separate location?

9. What time do you need to leave for the ceremony?

10. What is the total driving distance between prep, ceremony, and reception locations?

11. Is there venue parking for the videographer?

12. Are there any venue restrictions on cameras, lights, or audio recording?

13. Does the venue require proof of insurance or a vendor meal?

14. Who is the primary contact on the wedding day? Name, phone, and relationship.

15. Who is the secondary contact if the primary is unreachable?

Section 2: Creative vision and style

This section lets you match your editing style to what the couple actually wants.

16. How would you describe your ideal wedding film style? (Cinematic, documentary, light and airy, emotional, fun, vintage, etc.)

17. Are there example wedding films you love? Share links.

18. Are there example wedding films you do not like? Share links.

19. What tone do you want the film to have? (Romantic, upbeat, relaxed, dramatic)

20. Do you prefer a highlight film, a full documentary edit, or both?

21. What length do you envision for the highlight film?

22. Do you want the film set to music, dialogue and vows, or a mix?

23. Do you have a preference for background music genre?

24. Are there songs or artists you absolutely want used?

25. Are there songs or artists you do not want used?

26. Do you want the final film in a specific aspect ratio? (Widescreen, vertical for social, both)

27. Do you want social-media teaser clips included?

Section 3: Key moments and priorities

These are the shots that make or break the client’s reaction.

28. What are the three must-have moments you never want missed?

29. Are you planning a first look?

30. If yes, where and at what time?

31. Are there any surprise moments planned? (Flash mob, special dance, gift exchange)

32. Are there any cultural or religious traditions we should know about?

33. What time is the ceremony processional scheduled?

34. Will there be a unity candle, sand ceremony, wine box, or similar ritual?

35. Are you writing your own vows?

36. Do you want full ceremony audio captured?

37. What time are speeches and toasts scheduled?

38. Who is giving speeches? Names and relationships.

39. Is there a first dance, parent dance, or anniversary dance?

40. Are there any special exits planned? (Sparklers, bubbles, confetti, getaway car)

41. What time does the reception end?

Section 4: People and family dynamics

This section protects you from the most common wedding-day mistakes.

42. What are the full names and pronunciations of both partners?

43. Who are the parents and immediate family members? Names and relationships.

44. Are there divorced parents or sensitive family dynamics we should be aware of?

45. Are there family members who do not want to be filmed?

46. Who is in the wedding party? Names and roles.

47. Are there VIP guests we should keep an eye on? (Grandparents, special relatives)

48. Are there any children in the wedding party?

49. Do you have a wedding planner or day-of coordinator? Name, phone, email.

50. Who is your photographer? Company name and photographer name.

51. What is your photographer’s phone number and email?

52. What other vendors should we coordinate with? (DJ, florist, officiant, caterer)

Section 5: Music and audio

Audio is half the wedding film. Get this right.

53. Do you have a DJ or live band? Name and contact.

54. Will the officiant use a microphone?

55. Will the vows be spoken into a microphone?

56. Will speeches use a microphone?

57. Can we tap into the DJ or venue sound system for clean audio?

58. Do you want ambient natural audio or music-driven edits?

59. Are there any copyrighted songs you want in the final film? Note: licensing may apply.

60. Do you want raw ceremony audio delivered separately?

Section 6: Post-production and delivery

Set expectations before the wedding so the post-wedding conversation is easy.

61. What deliverables do you expect? (Highlight film, full ceremony, speeches, raw footage, teaser)

62. What is your preferred video length for the highlight film?

63. Do you want vertical or social-media versions?

64. How do you want to receive the final film? (Download link, USB, both)

65. What is your ideal turnaround time?

66. Do you want a first look or sneak peek before the full delivery?

67. Should we post a public trailer, or keep everything private?

68. Do you want the final film password-protected?

69. What is your mailing address for any physical deliverables?

70. Are there any editing restrictions? (No black-and-white, no drone, etc.)

Section 7: On-the-day access and logistics

These final questions prevent last-minute panic.

71. Are there any venue rules about where videographers can stand during the ceremony?

72. Will there be a videographer meal provided, or should we plan our own break?

73. Do you want us to film getting-ready audio or interviews?

74. Is there a backup indoor location in case of rain or bad weather?

75. Is there anything else about your wedding day, family, or vision you want us to know?

FAQ

When should I send a wedding videographer questionnaire?

Send it four to six weeks before the wedding. For destination weddings or multi-day events, send it eight weeks out.

What if the couple does not fill out the questionnaire?

Follow up once by email. If they still do not respond, send a shorter five-question confirmation form one week before the wedding.

Do I need to ask all 75 questions?

No. Remove sections that do not fit your package or shooting style. The full list is a safety net, not a requirement.

Can I use this questionnaire for wedding photography too?

Yes. Most questions work for both wedding photography and videography. The audio and music sections are videography-specific.

Frequently Asked Questions About Wedding Videographer Questionnaires

When should I send a wedding videographer questionnaire?

Send it four to six weeks before the wedding. This gives you time to follow up, build a timeline, and coordinate with other vendors.

What if the couple does not fill it out?

Send a friendly reminder one week after the deadline. If they still do not respond, call them directly. A missing questionnaire is a red flag for communication issues on the wedding day.

Do I need to ask all 75 questions?

No. Remove questions that do not apply to the couple or your package. The value is in covering every category, not in the total count.

Can I use this for wedding photography too?

Yes. Most questions apply to both wedding videographers and wedding photographers.

What is the best tool to send a wedding videography questionnaire?

Google Forms, Typeform, HoneyBook, Dubsado, and Jotform are all popular choices. Use whichever tool is already part of your workflow.
